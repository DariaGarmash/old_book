The Parable

  • Tags: 
  • book, 
  • different, 
  • very interesting
Print this page

Dying while on his deathbed, the Zen Master summoned his chief disciple, and from under the pillow took out a book. Each student was interested in this book because it is anyone and never allowed to look into it. Sometimes students at night peeped through the keyhole, as he read it. The teacher never left his room uncovered and would not allow anyone to enter the room without it. So that no one could see what was written in this book.

The teacher called his chief disciple and said: - Keep this book. This book has everything I taught. Take care of her, as you saw, I kept it. She gave me my teacher. Now I give it to you. This book - a legacy. Disciple took the book and threw it into the fire. All the others could not believe it. They were struck with terror. But the Master put his hand on the head of the disciple and blessed him. He said, "You got it. If you have kept the book, you will not be my disciple. In fact, in this book, there was nothing. It was empty. You threw it away - and you did the right thing. Do you understand my teaching: one should not follow any one else. Everyone should go their own way."
